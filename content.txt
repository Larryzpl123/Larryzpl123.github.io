=== INTRO ===
Class of 2027 at Shady Side Academy, boarding from Beijing. I build things at the intersection of **computational neuroscience**, **brain–computer interfaces**, and **history** — and I can't leave something *broken or missing* without building a fix.

=== ABOUT ===
I work at the intersection of **neuroscience, computing, and history**. I don't just learn things — I build them: a hippocampal CA1 microcircuit model, an EEG-controlled robotic hand, and a rating engine that turned my dorm's foosball table into a social hub.

What drives me is curiosity about the brain — how it computes, remembers, and might one day interface with machines. I'm just as at home reading about McCarthyism or the rise and fall of civilizations, and I care about using what I build to help people — from a scholarship-funded housing project for rural families to reading with kids.

=== CURRENTLY ===
Revising a **computational-neuroscience preprint** on simplifying hippocampal CA1 microcircuit models.

Building an **EEG-controlled robotic hand** — a first step toward brain–machine interfaces.

Co-President of the **Computing, Neuroscience, and Psychology clubs** at Shady Side Academy.

=== RESEARCH-INTERESTS ===
**Computational neuroscience** — biologically constrained microcircuit models, model simplification, and neural oscillations (theta / gamma).

**Brain–computer interfaces** — decoding motor imagery from EEG, and understanding *why* current pipelines fail.

**Memory and the hippocampus** — the CA1 circuit, and how structure gives rise to function.

I'm also drawn to the **history and philosophy of science** — how ideas, institutions, and technologies rise, fall, and get rebuilt.
